A parent-facing skill

Make every “why?” a moment together.

Wonder Together helps a grownup answer a child’s question with one clear image, a few things to notice, and one tiny way to try the idea together. Less output first, more shared attention.

Sample artifact

Why does the moon follow us when we drive?

For Leo, age 6

Core idea: far things barely move when you do.

Try it: thumb near your face, toy across the room, then step sideways.

A child asks a huge question. You get one warm image, a few things to notice, and a tiny way to try the idea together.

What it gives you

One image to look at, then just enough words to use it.

The default output is not a full packet. It is a compact visual explainer the adult can show, read, and try with the child. If the family wants more, the skill can expand into a story, longer explanation, game, or follow-up visual on request.

Core idea One or two child-sized sentences.
Explainer image One single-scene image or polished image prompt.
What to notice Two to four details the adult can point out.
Try it together One small, safe, parent-led thing to do.
Parent note A misconception, wording, tenderness, or safety cue.
