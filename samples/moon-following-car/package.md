## Wonder Together Visual Explainer

### Core idea

The moon looks like it follows your car because it is very, very far away. Close things rush past the window, but far things barely seem to move.

### Explainer image

Primary visual: `moon-parallax-explainer.png`, a single-scene parallax image
using Big Wonderer and Little Wonderer in the car, with a short hand-lettered
title and near/far diagram cues embedded into that same scene.

Optional support visuals:

- `story-scene.png`: cozy car-window story moment.
- `activity-thumb-test.png`: optional safe indoor parallax activity.
- `character-sheet.png`: reusable visual reference for the recurring pair.

### What to notice

- Close trees and signs slide quickly across the car window.
- The far moon barely seems to shift.
- Big Wonderer and Little Wonderer are looking at the same thing together.

### Try it together

Do the thumb test.

1. Hold your thumb up near your face.
2. Line it up with a lamp, toy, or picture across the room.
3. Move your head a little side to side.
4. Notice that your thumb seems to jump, while the far object barely moves.

Say: `Close things seem to move more when we move. Far things seem to move less.`

### Parent note

You do not have to erase the wonder. You can say: `It really does look like the moon is riding with us. Your eyes noticed something real about near and far things.`

### Want more?

Ask for a fuller packet if you want a read-aloud story, longer explanation, or
follow-up questions.

## Review Notes

- Character consistency: Big/Little Wonderer are consistent enough across character sheet, explainer, story scene, and activity visual. The pair reads more bear-like than abstract, which is the current v2 direction.
- Parent-child relationship: clear in all visuals through scale, proximity, and shared gaze.
- Strongest visual anchor: `moon-parallax-explainer.png`.
- Story scene: emotionally warm and useful as a read-aloud companion.
- Activity visual: clear and safe; it shows a room-scale alignment activity, not an outdoor or hazardous test.
- Copy/mascot risk: no direct Xiaohei copy, no poster framing. The small title
  should read as a field-guide header, not a title card. The v2 pair is close to
  a bear family, so future public use should choose that direction deliberately.

## Limitations

- The activity visual uses a teddy-like far toy on the shelf. It works as the far object, but future prompts could ask for a plain block, lamp, or toy rocket to avoid teddy-on-teddy ambiguity.
- Generated image text is not deterministic.
- The package is a demo artifact for evaluating the skill output and visual direction, not a final production asset set.
