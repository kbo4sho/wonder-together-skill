## Wonder Together Packet

### Core idea

The moon looks like it follows your car because it is very, very far away. Close things rush past the window, but far things barely seem to move.

### Story to read together

Little Wonderer leaned close to the car window while the road hummed under the tires. Mailboxes zipped by. Trees blurred into soft green streaks. Porch lights blinked and disappeared.

But the moon stayed right there.

Little Wonderer looked up at Big Wonderer. `The moon is following us again.`

Big Wonderer looked out too. `It really does look that way. Want to try a moon detective trick when we get home?`

Later, Big Wonderer held a small marker close to Little Wonderer's face and pointed to a toy across the room. `Line the marker up with the toy. Now move just a little to the side.`

Little Wonderer watched the near marker jump across the view while the far toy barely shifted.

`Close things seem to move a lot when we move,` Big Wonderer said. `Far things seem to move only a little. The moon is so far away that even when our car drives and drives, it barely changes place in the sky.`

On the next night drive, the moon still felt like company. Now Little Wonderer knew its secret.

### Explain it simply

When the car moves, close things like trees, signs, and houses pass by quickly because they are near you. The moon is much farther away than anything on the street. Your car ride is tiny compared with the distance to the moon, so the moon seems to stay in almost the same spot.

That close-and-far trick is called parallax.

### Try it together

Do the thumb test.

1. Hold your thumb up near your face.
2. Line it up with a lamp, toy, or picture across the room.
3. Move your head a little side to side.
4. Notice that your thumb seems to jump, while the far object barely moves.

Say: `Close things seem to move more when we move. Far things seem to move less.`

### Parent note

You do not have to erase the wonder. You can say: `It really does look like the moon is riding with us. Your eyes noticed something real about near and far things.`

### Follow-up questions

1. Does the sun ever look like it follows us too?
2. Why does the moon change shape?
3. How far away is the moon really?
