# Safety And Tenderness

Use this reference before drafting tender, hazardous, medical, legal, financial,
or emergency-adjacent visual explainers.

## Parent-Mediated Default

The adult is the user. Give the parent language, choices, and safe next moves.
Do not encourage unsupervised child use.

## Tender Topics

Tender topics include death, grief, illness, fear, bodies, identity, conflict, divorce, violence, disasters, and family instability.

Use direct, gentle language:

- Say `die` and `death` when death is the topic.
- Avoid euphemisms like `went to sleep` or `went away`.
- Do not make false promises.
- It is okay to say: `We do not know exactly when, and the grownups around you are caring for you right now.`
- Do not use broad reassurance like `most people live until they are very old` unless the parent supplied that context.

For tender topics:

- Keep the explainer short, direct, and parent-facing.
- Use `Parent note` when wording matters.
- Avoid games, experiments, diagrams, character scenes, and cheerful tidy endings unless the parent explicitly asks and the result remains kind.
- Skip generated images by default, or offer one optional gentle symbolic prompt such as a memory box, a tree through seasons, or a grownup holding a child's hand.

## Hazardous Topics

Do not suggest real-world testing with:

- outlets, plugs, cords, sockets, switches, breaker boxes, wires, or appliances
- fire, heat, flames, matches, lighters, stoves, or chemicals
- choking risks, sharp tools, heights, roads, vehicles, or water

Use safe substitutes:

- drawings
- pretend paths
- pointing from a normal distance
- adult-only descriptions
- already-safe everyday context
- conversation scripts

For electricity in the wall, adults may talk about a lamp that is already safely on, but should not plug, unplug, open, inspect, test, or touch electrical objects as part of the activity.

## Known Hard Questions

Black holes:

- Keep awe without horror.
- If the child asks what would happen to a person, answer honestly but briefly: a person could not survive past the event horizon.
- Do not dwell on bodily harm, stretching, crushing, being torn apart, or `spaghettification`.
- Prefer probes, maps, paths, light, telescope evidence, and safe-distance diagrams.

Medical, legal, financial, or emergency:

- Do not diagnose, instruct, or replace qualified help.
- Encourage the parent to contact an appropriate professional or emergency service when needed.
