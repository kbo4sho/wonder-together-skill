# Wonder Together Character System

Use this reference when a request needs consistent characters across Wonder Together visuals.

## Character Pair

Working labels:

- `Big Wonderer`
- `Little Wonderer`

They are a parent-child relational pair, not fixed human characters and not brand mascots. They may feel gently bear-adjacent, but they should not become literal bears, teddy toys, or animal mascots.

## Design Principles

- Gender-neutral.
- Nonhuman or abstract is allowed; soft bear-like cues are preferred for the current visual direction.
- Minimal diagram-actor style.
- Recognizable through silhouette, scale, posture, and shared attention.
- Useful in explanations without taking over the idea being explained.
- Warm enough for parent-child trust, simple enough for diagrams.

## Shape Language

Big Wonderer:

- Taller and broader.
- Rounded capsule or pebble body with a slightly squared base for steadiness.
- Tiny rounded ear nubs or soft corner-ear shapes.
- Very simple muzzle mark or nose dash, only if it remains subtle.
- Small offset dot-eyes or simple face marks.
- One short guiding arm or pointer gesture when needed, with mitten-like paw simplicity.
- Calm posture leaning slightly toward Little Wonderer.

Little Wonderer:

- Smaller and lighter.
- Rounded pebble body with a slightly lifted, curious posture.
- Tiny rounded ear nubs that echo Big Wonderer.
- Subtle muzzle mark or nose dash matching Big Wonderer.
- Tiny dot-eyes angled toward the concept being explored.
- Optional small scarf, satchel, or single mark only if it stays gender-neutral and repeatable.
- Often closer to the thing being wondered about.

Together:

- Show relationship through scale, proximity, shared gaze, and protective spacing.
- Big Wonderer should usually stand slightly behind or beside Little Wonderer, not overtake the scene.
- Avoid literal adult/child stereotypes, gendered clothing, eyelashes, hair styles, or family-role labels.

## Visual Style

- Hand-drawn dark ink linework.
- Mostly neutral palette: dark ink, warm cream paper, warm gray washes, and
  other low-saturation field-guide fills.
- Simple fills with light paper texture if available.
- Keep character fills quiet and neutral; put full-color emphasis on the
  explanation layer instead: arrows, paths, labels, active mechanisms, and
  safety signals.
- Sparse labels and arrows.
- Bear-adjacent cues should be readable at small sizes: ear nubs, warm rounded posture, tiny muzzle/nose mark, simple paw-like hands/feet.

## Usage Rules

- Use the pair to anchor attention, scale, or emotional safety.
- Keep the science/history/activity content as the main subject.
- In diagrams, make the pair small and clear rather than expressive and central.
- Reuse the same silhouettes, face marks, and relative scale across an image set.
- Keep them out of tender-topic literal imagery unless they appear only as a gentle, symbolic parent-child presence.

## Avoid

- Xiaohei-style blob characters or direct imitation of any existing blob-figure identity.
- A cute mascot narrator who replaces the parent-child practice.
- Literal bear anatomy, realistic fur, claws, paw pads, tails, animal costumes, teddy-bear stitching, plush-toy seams, or toy-like button eyes.
- Gendered names, clothing, hair, or eyelashes.
- Human-specific identity markers unless the user explicitly requests a family portrait.
- Brand posters, title cards, or character merchandise poses.

## Prompt Snippet

Use this wording when asking for generated visuals:

`Include the Wonder Together recurring pair: Big Wonderer and Little Wonderer. They are gender-neutral, nonhuman minimal diagram actors with gentle bear-adjacent cues: rounded pebble/capsule silhouettes, tiny rounded ear nubs, tiny dot-eyes, a subtle muzzle or nose dash, mitten-like paw simplicity, dark hand-drawn linework, and a mostly neutral ink/cream/warm-gray palette with muted field-guide fills. Keep the pair visually quiet; reserve clear full-color accents for the explainer layer, such as arrows, paths, labels, active mechanisms, and safety cues. Big Wonderer is taller and steady, standing protectively beside or behind Little Wonderer. Little Wonderer is smaller and curious, looking toward the concept. Keep them subtle and consistent; do not make them mascots, humans, literal bears, teddy toys, blob-character copies, posters, or title-card figures.`
